The Problem

It is no secret that technological advances have created a plethora of dangers to kids today. oThere are many kid-protecting solutions that do great work to make technology safer for kids like child-friendly smart phones and apps that monitor user activity. Now the problem is that social media is such an integral part of our society, and parents worry about what their kids are being exposed to online. Whether it be nudity and pornography, graphic violence, vulgarity, cyber-bullying, or at worst, sexual predators who target children, there are plenty of things that children should not be seeing, in any amount.

Our Solution

The only current solutions for this problem are to either block social media altogether or to try to constantly monitor the user's activity (which is difficult and sometimes impossible with current solutions). Now, Safe Social is creating a way to filter social media platforms so that everything showing up on a kid’s screen has been pre-screened by an accurate and continually-learning AI model to ensure the child is not exposed to unwanted material and conversations. The kid will be able to connect with friends and consume wholesome content on social media platforms like Instagram and YouTube, while being strongly protected behind the scenes by safe social.

This Solution is much more appealing to kids—especially teenagers—and parents. At the same time, it is much safer than apps that strictly monitor content after the fact. Again, kids SHOULD NOT be seeing these things at all. It is also, in some ways, safer than just blocking social media completely or getting a kid a dummy phone, which causes some kids to feel overly restricted and socially rejected at school.

Important Note

Please rest assured that we also see the vital importance of monitoring and setting screen time limits for social media apps. Our app will give parents more control, ease, and flexibility in setting these protocols and boundaries into their child’s social media experience. Our Goal is to give parents confidence and ability so they can be the one guiding their kids' online experience.
